Horse Information

Safety Tips

Do not leave halters on in the pasture or the stall.   You wouldn’t believe what a horse can get its halter hung on, including its own foot (while scratching its face).  Worst case scenario is death from a broken neck.

Breast straps are functional.  They’re not just for decoration.  Flopping, loose breast straps are not holding the saddle in place, so adjust the straps where the breast strap fits snugly so the saddle will not slide back.

Don’t dismount immediately after riding.  When you return to the barn (or trailer) after a ride, spend time just sitting on your horse to discipline him in patience, which will actually help prevent him from becoming barnsour.

Never say “whoa” unless you mean it.  To slow down, talk to him in soothing tones and use a different verbal command than your command for halting, such as “easy” or “walk”.  When you do say “whoa”, MAKE HIM WHOA!!

Horse Maintenance

Daily Care

Daily care for a horse involves a routine that ensures their health, comfort, and well-being. This includes feeding them high-quality hay or grain twice a day, along with constant access to clean water. Grooming is essential to keep their coat clean and to check for injuries or irritations, while hoof care helps prevent infections like thrush. . Regular turnout or exercise keeps them physically and mentally healthy. Cleaning their stall or paddock daily maintains hygiene and reduces the risk of illness. Observing your horse’s behavior and physical condition each day helps catch any signs of illness early, ensuring they stay happy and healthy.

Veterinary Care

Veterinary care is a vital part of maintaining a horse’s overall health and preventing illness. Regular check-ups help monitor the horse’s condition and ensure vaccinations and deworming schedules are up to date. A vet should also examine any signs of injury, illness, or lameness promptly to prevent complications. Dental care, usually done annually, is important for proper chewing and nutrition. By working closely with a veterinarian, horse owners can ensure their horse stays healthy, comfortable, and able to perform at their best.

Nutrition

Horse nutrition plays a crucial role in maintaining their health, energy, and overall well-being. The foundation of a horse’s diet is good-quality forage, such as hay or pasture, which provides essential fiber for healthy digestion. Depending on their age, activity level, and health status, horses may also need grains or specialized supplements to meet their nutritional needs. Access to clean, fresh water at all times is vital, as horses can easily become dehydrated. A well-balanced diet helps support strong bones, a shiny coat, and a healthy immune system, keeping the horse fit and thriving.
